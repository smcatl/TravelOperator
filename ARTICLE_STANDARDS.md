# TravelOperator Article Standards
Last updated: April 2026

## Voice
- Team perspective ONLY — author is always "TravelOperator Team"
- NEVER use first person singular ("I tested", "my experience", "I found")
- Always "we", "our team", "our testing"
- Credential: our team has reviewed thousands of software tools and managed thousands of locations across travel agencies, tour operators, and hospitality businesses
- NEVER mention SkyYield, Stosh, Nadim, or any individual names
- NEVER mention being paid, earning commissions, or receiving referral payments in article body text
- Use real numbers: actual costs, real deployment counts, specific tools
- Be honest about cons even on tools we recommend
- Short paragraphs — 2-3 sentences max
- Never: "in conclusion", "in summary", "it's worth noting", "in today's fast-paced world"

## Required Elements Checklist
- [ ] Quick stats box (4 stats: rating, price, key metric, best for)
- [ ] Verdict/bottom line box at top
- [ ] Table of contents (auto-generated by sidebar -- just use h2/h3 with id attributes)
- [ ] Emoji on every h2 heading
- [ ] At least 2 callout boxes (blue for tips, orange for warnings, green for recommendations)
- [ ] Pros/cons box on all reviews
- [ ] Pricing table (HTML table, not markdown)
- [ ] Who it's for / who should skip it
- [ ] Affiliate CTA with specific action text (no commission/payment disclosure in body)
- [ ] Further Reading section (3 internal links)

## SEO Rules
- Title: 50-60 chars, include "2026", include primary keyword
- Description: 150-160 chars, include primary keyword naturally
- Primary keyword in first paragraph
- At least 3 internal links per article
- All h2/h3 must have id attributes for TOC

## Affiliate Link Rules
- Redirect format: https://traveloperator.io/recommends/toolname
- Redirects auto-generate from affiliates.json at build time — edit affiliates in admin portal, NOT in vercel.json
- CTA text must be specific: "Start Free 30-Day Trial" not "Try it"
- Primary CTA: 3 placements for the main reviewed tool (after intro, mid-article, end)
- NEVER disclose commission rates, payment, or "affiliate link" labels in article body
- The /affiliate-disclaimer page handles FTC compliance — do NOT repeat it per-article

### Cross-linking (REQUIRED for monetization depth)
Every article must naturally reference 2-4 additional affiliate partners beyond
the primary tool. These are NOT extra CTAs — they are contextual mentions that
link to the partner's review or /recommends/ page. Patterns:

1. **Comparison mentions**: "Unlike [OtherTool](/recommends/othertool), [Primary] handles X differently..."
2. **Integration callouts**: "[Primary] pairs well with [Tool](/recommends/tool) for [use case]"
3. **Alternatives section**: "If [Primary] isn't right, consider [Tool A](/recommends/toola) for X or [Tool B](/recommends/toolb) for Y"
4. **Further Reading links**: link to other reviews/guides that feature affiliate partners
5. **Stack recommendations**: "Most operators pair this with [Tool](/recommends/tool) and [Tool](/recommends/tool2)"

Rules for cross-links:
- Must feel natural and helpful to the reader, not forced
- Each cross-linked partner gets 1-2 contextual mentions (NOT big CTA buttons)
- Only link partners that are genuinely relevant to the article's topic
- Use the site's /recommends/ redirect URL, not raw affiliate URLs
- Reference the site's affiliates.json for available partners to cross-link

## Article Structures

### Review
What Is [Tool]? → Our Testing → Feature Breakdown →
Pricing → Pros & Cons → Who It's For → Final Verdict → CTA

### Comparison
Quick Verdict box → Overview (why they're different) →
Head-to-Head Table → Deep Dive by Category →
Who Should Use Each → Final Verdict → CTA

### Roundup Guide
What to Look For → Quick Comparison Table →
#1 Pick (detailed, 400+ words) → #2-5 (150 words each) →
Full Comparison Table → Our Recommendation → CTA

## Prohibited Content
- NEVER mention earning money from affiliate links, referral fees, or commissions in article text
- NEVER mention SkyYield, any parent company, or individual team member names
- NEVER use "I" or first-person singular — always team voice ("we", "our team")
- NEVER reference specific revenue, income, or earnings from recommendations
- NEVER use crypto/blockchain terminology (tokens, coins, epochs, mining, staking)
- NEVER use "in conclusion", "in summary", "it's worth noting", "without further ado"
- NEVER claim exact numbers of reviews/tests unless verifiable — use "thousands of tools reviewed"
- All experience claims use "thousands" (thousands of locations, thousands of tools, etc.)

## ClickFunnels® Content Rules (MANDATORY — Affiliate Agreement Compliance)

ClickFunnels® has strict affiliate agreement terms. Every piece of content
mentioning ClickFunnels® must follow these rules or the site risks commission
forfeiture and account termination.

### Trademark rule
Always write "ClickFunnels®" with the registered trademark symbol — every
single instance, no exceptions. Never write "Clickfunnels" or "clickfunnels".

### Disclosure rule
Every page promoting ClickFunnels® must display this exact text, plainly visible
(not in a footer, not in small font):

"Disclosure: TravelOperator Team is an independent ClickFunnels® Affiliate, not an employee.
TravelOperator Team receives referral payments from ClickFunnels®. The opinions expressed here
are the team's own and are not official statements of ClickFunnels® or its parent
company, Etison LLC."

### Non-disparagement rule — CRITICAL
The ClickFunnels® affiliate agreement prohibits negative comments about
ClickFunnels® AND its competitors. This means:

NEVER publish with a ClickFunnels® affiliate link present:
- Any head-to-head comparison where ClickFunnels® loses
- Any article where GoHighLevel, Kajabi, Kartra, or any funnel builder is
  positioned as better than ClickFunnels®
- Any article listing ClickFunnels® weaknesses as the main angle

SAFE content formats:
- Standalone review: "ClickFunnels® Review: Is It Worth It for Operators?"
- Use-case guides: "How to Build a Client Onboarding Funnel with ClickFunnels®"
- Best-of roundups where ClickFunnels® is the recommended winner in its category

### Which site to publish on
ClickFunnels® content should NOT go on OperatorStack where GoHighLevel is
the primary affiliate — the non-disparagement clause creates conflict.
Best fit is EcommerceOperator.io once launched, or a standalone
funnel-builder content cluster.

### Income claims
Never imply ClickFunnels® guarantees income. Always include:
"These were our results. Your results will vary based on a variety of factors
including your education, effort, and market factors. There is no guarantee
you will make any money."
